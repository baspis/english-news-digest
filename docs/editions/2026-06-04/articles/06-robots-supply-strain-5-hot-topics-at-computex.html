Grammar focus

Passive voice in news writing

Use be + past participle to focus on events/results rather than agents (e.g., has been fueled, have been raised, are banned).

Concerns have been raised over supplies of chipmaking materials.

ニュースでは受動態で「誰が」より「何が起きたか・何が懸念されるか」を前面に出す。能動態より客観的でフォーマルな印象になる。

Participial phrases for extra information

Use -ing/-ed phrases or 'with + noun + participle/adjective' to add context without new clauses.

Referring to the emerging world of AI systems, ... / with chip factories taking at least three years to build.

分詞構文や with 構文で、主節を重くせずに背景・理由・付随状況を追加できる。長いニュース文で頻出。

Non-restrictive relative clauses

Use commas (or dashes) with which/who clauses to add parenthetical explanation.

China, which considers the self-ruled island its territory, could also pose a threat.

カンマやダッシュで挟んだ関係代名詞節は補足説明。なくても文の骨格は成立する。地政学・技術説明で多用される。

Causative make + object + complement
