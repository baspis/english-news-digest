Grammar focus

Participial phrases as adjuncts

Present participles (-ing) can follow a main clause to add result, manner, or extra detail (e.g., emphasizing..., ending..., making...).

Apple unveiled new AI advances, emphasizing a focus on privacy.

現在分詞句を主節の後に置くと、付随する状況・結果・方法を補足できる。ニュース記事で背景や意図を簡潔に足すときによく使われる。

Non-restrictive relative clauses

Use commas + which/who to add extra information that is not essential to identify the noun.

Siri AI, which was introduced at the start of WWDC, has been highly anticipated.

カンマで区切ったwhich/who節は補足説明であり、なくても文の骨格は成立する。固有名やイベントの背景を足すビジネス記事の基本パターン。

Contrast connectors: While, Rather, in contrast to

While can show contrast (not just time); Rather contrasts with a previous idea; in contrast to compares two things.

While Siri was launched in 2011, it fell behind other voice assistants.

Whileは「〜だが」、Ratherは「むしろ」、in contrast toは「〜とは対照的に」。記事では賛否や方針の違いを整理するのに頻出。

Direct quotation in news reporting

News articles mix reporting verbs (said, told, announced) with direct quotes in quotation marks, sometimes split across sentences.

Cook told the audience he is “deeply grateful to have been on this journey with you.”

報道文ではsaid/told/announcedと直接引用を組み合わせ、発言者の言葉をそのまま伝える。引用が文をまたぐこともある。
