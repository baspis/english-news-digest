Japan Today World · 2026-06-03

Grammar focus

Reported speech in news writing

Use patterns like 'X quoted/quoted A as -ing' and 'Y told AFP' to attribute claims without stating them as fact.

Axios quoted Trump as shouting at Netanyahu in the call on Monday.

ニュース英語では、直接の事実断言を避け、quote ... as -ing や told AFP などで「誰がそう述べたか」を明示する。reportedly も同様に情報源を留保する。

Unreal conditional (if it weren't for)

Use 'if it weren't for' + noun to describe a hypothetical situation contrary to reality.

You'd be in prison if it weren't for me.

if it weren't for は「～がなければ（実際にはあるので結果は違う）」という仮定法。You'd = You would で非現実の結果を表す。政治引用でも攻撃的な脅しの表現として使われる。

Participle clauses for reason (fearing / when asked)

A present participle or past participle phrase can replace a full clause with 'because' or 'when'.

Trump unleashed a tirade, fearing it would undermine talks with Tehran.

分詞構文は理由や時を簡潔に示す。fearing = because he feared、when asked = when they were asked のように、主語が前後で一致する場合に使う。

Relative clause chains

Multiple relative clauses (that/which/who) can stack to add layers of detail in one sentence.

Trump wants a way out of a war that has badly hit the U.S. economy, which will decide if his party keeps control of Congress.

関係代名詞 that（制限用法）と which（非制限用法）を連鎖させ、一文に背景・因果・詳細を詰め込むのは英語ニュースの特徴。日本語訳では文を分けた方が自然なことが多い。
