Grammar focus

Participial phrases for results

Use -ing/-ed phrases after a main clause to add a result or extra detail (e.g., showing..., marking..., offsetting...).

Hiring has bounced back this year, showing resilience in the face of economic uncertainty.

主節のあとに分詞構文を置き、結果や付加情報を簡潔に足す報道文体。日本語の「〜して／〜しながら」に相当する。

Reporting clause placement

In news writing, the source (e.g., the Labor Department said / said Ryan Nunn) often comes at the end, or the speaker is inserted mid-sentence.

Employers added 172,000 jobs in May, the Labor Department reported Friday.

引用元や情報源を文末や文中に置くことで、まず事実を伝え、出所は後から示す英語のニュース構文。

Contrast with despite / but

Use despite + noun/gerund or but to contrast two ideas (strong jobs vs. frustration, hiring vs. modest wages).

And despite the pickup in hiring, wage gains were modest.

despite + 名詞／動名詞で「〜にもかかわらず」と逆接の対比を作る。記事全体の「好調な雇用 vs 国民の不満」構造の要。

Relative clauses (who/which/that)

Use who/which/that to add information about people, policies, or economic factors in long news sentences.

President Donald Trump who has taken a beating in the polls over the surging gasoline prices that followed U.S. and Israeli attacks on Iran.

関係代名詞で人物・物・政策に説明を重ねる。経済記事では背景説明が長くなるため、先行詞と節の対応を意識する必要がある。
