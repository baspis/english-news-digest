Grammar focus

Present/Past Perfect Passive in news

Use has/had been + past participle to report completed actions with relevance to the present or to a past reference point.

An 88-year-old man has been arrested. / She had been struck multiple times.

犯罪報道では、現在完了受動態で「逮捕された」という最新の結果を、過去完了受動態で「その時点より前に〜されていた」という被害の経緯を伝える。

on suspicion of / be accused of + gerund

Both are followed by a gerund (-ing), not an infinitive, to describe the alleged crime.

arrested on suspicion of killing / is accused of killing

どちらも「〜した疑いで」という意味だが、of の後は必ず動名詞。確定前の容疑を慎重に述べる報道・法曹の表現。

According to X, ... , Y reported

News often attributes information to a source at the start (According to...) and names the media outlet at the end (... Corp reported).

According to police, he is accused of killing..., Nagano Broadcasting Corp reported.

情報源を文頭と文末の両方で示し、客観性と出典の明確さを確保するニュース英語の典型構文。

by + -ing (means/method)

Use by + gerund to explain how something was done, often with with + instrument.

by beating her with a baseball bat and stabbing her with a knife

by + 動名詞で「〜することによって」と手段を示し、with で道具を補足する。複数の方法は and で並列できる。
