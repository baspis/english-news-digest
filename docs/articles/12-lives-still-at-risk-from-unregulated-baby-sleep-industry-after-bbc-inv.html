Grammar focus

Reporting with passive (was told / was revealed)

News writing often uses passive voice to report information without focusing on who said it: 'X was told', 'it was revealed'.

a parliament debate was told last night

報道では「〜と伝えられた」「〜が明らかになった」のように受動態を使い、情報源より内容を前面に出す。

call for / urge + action

Both mean to publicly request something, often followed by nouns or 'to' + verb.

MPs are urging the government to set out a timeline

call for と urge は「〜を求める」という政治・報道で頻出の表現。urge は to 不定詞、call for は名詞句が続くことが多い。

Reduced relative clauses (-ing / -ed)

Relative clauses can be shortened: 'people who work' → 'people working'; 'advice that is backed' → 'advice backed by'.

everyone providing paid support to families

関係代名詞＋be動詞を省略し、現在分詞・過去分詞で名詞を後ろから修飾する形。ニュースで非常に多い。

make + object + adjective

Use 'make' + noun + adjective to show that something is required or caused to become a certain way.

legislation to make training and background checks compulsory

make + 目的語 + 形容詞で「〜を…にする」。ここでは訓練と身元調査を義務化する、という意味。
