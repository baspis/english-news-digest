Grammar focus

Approximation with 'if not'

Use 'X, if not Y' to suggest the real number may be even higher than X.

Hundreds, if not thousands, of people's homes are being taken over every week.

「数百、いや数千に及ぶかもしれない」のように、前の数字を上回る可能性を示すニュース記事でよく使われる表現。

Passive voice in crime reporting

Passive structures (be + past participle) focus on victims and actions rather than agents.

Drug users are exploited by gangs who move in and refuse to leave.

犯罪報道では受動態で被害者や事件の内容に焦点を当て、加害者は by 句や関係節で後から示すことが多い。

Reporting clauses with inversion

After a long quote, the reporting verb and subject can follow: '...,' said Name.

"We suspect hundreds of properties are being cuckooed every week," said Dent.

長い直接引用のあとに said + 人名 を置く倒置は英語新聞の定型パターン。

Participle phrases for extra detail
