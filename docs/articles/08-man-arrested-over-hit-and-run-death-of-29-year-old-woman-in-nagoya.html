Grammar focus

Passive voice in crime headlines

Headlines often use passive voice without auxiliaries: Subject + past participle (+ optional agent phrase with by).

Man arrested over hit-and-run death

犯罪ニュースの見出しでは、誰が逮捕したかより事件そのものを前面に出すため、受動態の短い形がよく使われます。

on suspicion of + noun/-ing

Use 'on suspicion of' to state the alleged offense when reporting an arrest.

arrested a 21-year-old man on suspicion of negligent driving

逮捕報道で「〜の疑いで」という公式な言い方をする定型表現です。

According to + source

Place 'According to [authority/media]' at the start of a sentence to attribute information.

According to police, Kei Tsunoda ... is accused of hitting Akane Murata

情報の出所（警察・当局・メディア）を明示するニュース英語の基本パターンです。

while + past continuous

Use 'while + was/were + -ing' to show one action was in progress when another happened.

hit Murata while he was making a right turn

ある動作の最中に別の出来事が起きたことを、背景動作を過去進行形で表します。
