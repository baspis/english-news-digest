Grammar focus

Inversion for emphasis

Place complements or adverbials before the verb (e.g. 'On the launch pad stands...') to create dramatic, journalistic emphasis.

and on the launch pad stands the biggest rocket ever made.

場所や補語を文頭に置き動詞を続ける倒置で、場面描写や強調効果を出す新聞文体。

Reduced relative clauses (-ing/-ed)

Use present/past participles after nouns instead of full relative clauses to pack information densely.

engineers who have achieved something that's never been done before → controlled by a talismanic individual, that has captured the world's imagination

関係代名詞節を分詞（-ing/-ed）や形容詞句に短縮し、情報を詰め込む記事特有の書き方。

Contrast with but / even though / despite

Layer contrasts with 'but', 'even though', 'despite', and 'and yet' to balance hype against scepticism.

But even the most optimistic estimates value this part of SpaceX at around $300bn - less than 20% of SpaceX's $1.75trn target valuation.

but / even though / despite / and yet を重ね、期待と懸念を対比させる論説記事の論理展開。

Embedded questions in reporting

Use indirect questions and rhetorical questions after reporting verbs (says, asks) without inversion.

Robert Armstrong asks: "Do you really own something you can't control?"

報道動詞の後に間接疑問や修辞疑問を続け、論点を読者に投げかける。
